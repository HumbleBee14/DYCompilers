# Core Resources

## Books & References
- *Compilers: Principles, Techniques, and Tools* (Aho, Lam, Sethi, Ullman).
- *Engineering a Compiler* (Cooper & Torczon).
- *Modern Compiler Implementation* (Appel) — pick the language edition you prefer.
- *Crafting Interpreters* (Nystrom) — approachable, especially for parsing and VMs.
- *Optimizing Compilers for Modern Architectures* (Allen & Kennedy).

## Courses & Lectures
- Stanford CS143 (video lectures, assignments).
- MIT 6.035 (Compiler Design) lecture notes and labs.
- University of Waterloo CS444 open courseware for advanced passes.
- LLVM Developer Conference talks (YouTube) on optimisation and MLIR.

## ML Compiler Specific
- MLIR official tutorials and the Toy dialect walkthrough.
- TVM documentation and the "Introduction to Relay" guide.
- OpenXLA StableHLO specifications and ecosystem blogs.
- Glow compiler whitepaper, Tensor Comprehensions papers.

## Tools & Libraries
- LLVM/Clang toolchain.
- MLIR (part of LLVM).
- Cranelift (Rust-based backend) for exploring alternative IRs.
- ANTLR or Lark for grammar experimentation.
- pytest / googletest for compiler regression suites.

## Communities & Discussion
- LLVM discourse forums & mailing lists.
- Compilers channel on Zig, Rust, and C++ community Discords.
- Papers We Love (compiler / PL sessions).
- TVM and MLIR community meetings.

Keep enriching this document with new articles, videos, and papers as you progress.
